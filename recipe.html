Emergency Meals

Quick and nutritious meals that can be prepared in crisis situations with minimal resources.

Quick Pantry Lentil Stew
25 min 4 servings
Ingredients
  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp dried onion flakes
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 vegetable bouillon cube
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups water
Instructions

Rinse lentils and combine with 4 cups water in a pot; bring to a boil. Add tomatoes (with juices), onion flakes, garlic powder, and bouillon cube. Season with salt and pepper, reduce heat, and simmer 20 min until lentils are soft. Taste, adjust seasoning, and serve hot.

Rapid Relief Chili
15 min 4 servings
Ingredients
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili seasoning
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt to taste
Instructions

Combine beans, tomato sauce, chili seasoning, and oregano in a pot. Stir to mix, bring to a boil over medium heat. Reduce heat and simmer 10 min, stirring occasionally. Season with salt and serve.

Potato Bombs
10 min 4 servings
Ingredients
  • 4 medium potatoes
  • ½ cup shredded cheese
  • 4 tbsp canned beans or cooked veggies (optional)
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil or butter (optiona)
Instructions

Cook rice per package instructions (usually 5 min). In a small pot, heat beans with onion powder, garlic powder, cumin, and a pinch of salt until warmed (2–3 min). Stir beans into cooked rice and heat together 1–2 min. Drizzle with oil if desired and serve.

Swift Tomato Pasta
15 min 4 servings
Ingredients
  • 8 oz pasta (any shape)
  • 1 can (10 oz) tomato soup
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water (for cooking pasta)
Instructions

Boil pasta in salted water per package (about 8 min); drain. In the same pot, heat tomato soup with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and a pinch of salt until bubbling. Add cooked pasta, toss to coat, and heat 1–2 min. Serve immediately.

Instant Veggie Ramen
10 min 4 serving
Ingredients

Budget-Friendly

Affordable and nutritious meals that stretch your budget without compromising on taste or nutrition.

One-Pot Chickpea Tomato Rice
20 min 4 servings
Ingredients
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes (undrained)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 2 cups water or broth
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions

Heat oil in a pot over medium; toast rice 1 min. Add tomatoes (with juice), chickpeas, water, and spices; bring to a boil. Cover, reduce heat to low, and simmer 15 min. Turn off heat; let sit covered 5 min. Fluff, season, and serve.

Bean & Cheese Roll-Ups
10 min 4 servings
Ingredients
  • 4 tortillas
  • 1 can (15 oz) beans (black or pinto), drained and mashed
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
Instructions

Lay tortillas flat; spread ¼ cup mashed beans on each. Sprinkle ¼ cup cheese over beans; roll up tightly. Heat a skillet over medium; grill roll-ups 2 min per side until cheese melts. Slice in half and serve.

Spicy Bean Tacos
15 min 4 serving
Ingredients

  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder

Instructions

Heat oil in a pan over medium; add beans and spices, cook 2–3 min until heated. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave 20 sec. Divide bean mixture among tortillas. Top with lettuce, tomato, cheese, or salsa and serve.

Simple Sugar Cookies
20 min 24 servings
Ingredients
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
Instructions

Preheat oven to 375 °F (190 °C). Cream butter and sugar; mix in egg and vanilla. Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt until dough forms. Scoop tablespoon-sized balls onto a baking sheet; bake 9–11 min until edges are lightly golden. Cool on a wire rack.
